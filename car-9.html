When comparing electric, diesel, and petrol vehicles, several elements come into play. The engine configuration differs among these vehicle types, affecting their performance and efficiency. The power band and redline also vary based on the fuel type used. Moreover, the cooling system, including the fan, radiator, and thermostat, adapts to the specific requirements of electric, diesel, and petrol engines. Additionally, brake fluid and brake linings need to be tailored to the demands of each vehicle type, particularly considering environmental factors like rain and snow. In essence, each fuel option has its own set of advantages and limitations that impact the overall performance and environmental footprint of the car.
